# Finance explainer outline skeleton

Target: 18–30 min read, 4–6k words, ≥ 4 figures, ≥ 3 worked examples. One self-contained mechanism explained so well a beginner fully gets it. Less exhaustive than a deep-dive — no long real-markets catalog — but the same accessible-then-deep arc.

## Section list

1. **TL;DR callout** (`> [!important]` with a `**TL;DR**` lead) — at the very top. ≤ 5 bullets.
2. **Hook (`## Introduction`)** — a phenomenon the reader has noticed ("Your savings account pays 0.5% but inflation is 3% — so you're *losing* money. Here's the mechanism."). End by referencing the mental-model figure.
3. **Embed the primary diagram** right after the hook.
4. **`## Foundations: what you need first`** — 1–3 sub-sections covering the minimum background, every term defined from zero, with a concrete analogy. Include the simplest worked example here.
5. **`## The problem / why it exists`** — show the pain or the question without the answer yet. One worked example that *motivates* the mechanism.
6. **`## How it actually works`** — the core explanation, laddered: simplest case → realistic wrinkle. The central worked example lives here, every step shown, numbers matching the figure.
7. **`## The trade-offs`** — quantify costs/risks vs benefits. A "simple view vs what actually happens" table.
8. **`## Common misconceptions`** — 3–5 wrong beliefs corrected with the why.
9. **`## Summary`** — 5–8 bullets; the last is the single most important takeaway. (This is the one place a short recap is allowed — keep it to takeaways, not throat-clearing.)

## Figure plan defaults

- Figure 1 (mental model): the data/money flow or before-after that motivates the topic.
- Figure 2: a `cash-flow-timeline` or `payoff`/`yield-curve` chart for the core mechanism.
- Figure 3: a comparison `matrix` or `before-after`.
- Figure 4: a memory/structure or worked-example illustration. (Add more wherever an abstraction lacks a nearby figure.)

## Required patterns to include

- The TL;DR callout at the top.
- A Foundations section that assumes zero background.
- ≥ 3 worked numeric examples with round numbers, every step shown.
- One comparison table.
- Common misconceptions section.
