# Finance concept (primer) outline skeleton

Target: 10–18 min read, 2.2–3.5k words, ≥ 3 figures, ≥ 2 worked examples. A focused "what is X and why should I care" primer. Short, but still TL;DR-first, still foundations-first, still grounded in a worked example.

## Section list

1. **TL;DR callout** (`> [!important]` with a `**TL;DR**` lead) — at the very top. ≤ 4 bullets: what it is, why it matters, the one thing to remember.
2. **Hook** — one or two sentences placing the concept in the reader's world. Reference the mental-model figure.
3. **The mental-model figure** — right after the hook.
4. **`## The basics: what it is`** — define the term from zero with a plain-English analogy, then the precise definition. Include the simplest worked example.
5. **`## How it works`** — one full step-by-step walkthrough with small numbers; the figure's numbers match.
6. **`## Why it matters / where you meet it`** — the concrete situations a beginner encounters it; the risk/cost honestly stated.
7. **`## Common misconceptions`** — 2–4 quick corrections. (Optional for the shortest primers, but preferred.)

## Figure plan defaults

- Figure 1 (mental model): the one picture that captures the concept.
- Figure 2: a `cash-flow-timeline` / `payoff` / `yield-curve` chart, or a `before-after`.
- Figure 3: a small comparison `matrix` or a `tree` placing the concept among its siblings.

## Required patterns to include

- The TL;DR callout at the top.
- A basics/foundations section assuming zero background.
- ≥ 2 worked numeric examples with round numbers.
- At least one figure within 30 lines of every prose abstraction.
